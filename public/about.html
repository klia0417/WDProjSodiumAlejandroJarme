About TWICE

TWICE is a South Korean girl group formed by JYP Entertainment in 2015 through the survival show SIXTEEN. Their debut single "Like Ooh-Ahh" introduced the group’s signature bright, catchy sound and colorful, energetic concepts.

The name TWICE reflects their goal to capture fans’ hearts “once” through their music and “twice” through their visuals and performances. With nine talented members — Nayeon, Jeongyeon, Momo, Sana, Jihyo, Mina, Dahyun, Chaeyoung, and Tzuyu — TWICE quickly became a global phenomenon.

Known for their cheerful, vibrant image and synchronized dance routines, TWICE has not only dominated the K-pop scene but also influenced fashion, social media trends, and international pop culture. Their fanbase, called ONCE, is a testament to their worldwide impact.

Fun Facts

  • They are multilingual, speaking Korean, Japanese, and some English, reflecting their international appeal.
  • The group’s official light stick is called the Candy Bong, popular at concerts worldwide.
  • They actively participate in variety shows, online content, and philanthropic events, strengthening their bond with fans.
  • TWICE has consistently collaborated with global brands like Pepsi, Shiseido, and Puma.

Awards & Achievements

  • First female Korean act to hold a Japanese dome tour.
  • Multiple Daesangs (Grand Prizes) at major Korean award shows.
  • First K-pop girl group to have three music videos surpass 400 million views.
  • Consistently ranks among the top-selling K-pop girl groups of all time.
  • First K-pop girl group to enter the US Billboard Hot 100.
  • First K-pop female group to earn a platinum RIAA certification.
  • Multiple MAMA awards, including Fans’ Choice Female Top 10.

Milestone Timeline (2015–2025)

  • 2015 Oct: Debut with The Story Begins and "Like Ooh-Ahh".
  • 2016 Apr: Released Page Two with the breakout hit "Cheer Up".
  • 2016–2017: Iconic songs "TT" and "Knock Knock" define their energetic style.
  • 2017 Jun: Japanese debut with #TWICE, expanding their international presence.
  • 2018–2019: Japanese dome tours, record-breaking album sales, and consistent chart dominance.
  • 2019 Sep: Released Feel Special, showcasing a more mature concept.
  • 2020: Released Eyes Wide Open; members actively participate in creative direction.
  • 2021: "The Feels" enters the US Billboard Hot 100; released Formula of Love: O+T=<3.
  • 2022: Between 1&2 becomes their first million-selling EP.
  • 2023 Mar: Launched the "Ready to Be World Tour" for their 12th EP.
  • 2024 Feb: With You-th becomes No.1 on the Billboard 200.
  • 2024–2025: 14th EP Strategy charts in US Top 10; major world tour.
  • 2025 Jul: Fourth Korean studio album This Is For released for 10th anniversary.
  • 2025 Jul: Began the "This Is For World Tour" across Asia, Australia, and more.
  • 2025 Aug: First K-pop girl group to headline Lollapalooza Chicago.
  • 2025 Oct: Special anniversary album TEN: The Story Goes On released.
  • 2025 Oct: Sold-out 10th anniversary fan event “10VE UNIVERSE (Love Universe)” celebrated.
  • Late 2025: Earned platinum RIAA certification for "Strategy"; won multiple major awards.
